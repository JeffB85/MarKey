Trademark Clearance


Before you start using and registering a new trademark, we recommend that you first check the availability of the trademark. Only then will you know whether you can use your brand undisturbed.

During an investigation, we check whether there are older registrations of identical or similar trademarks in the relevant trademark registers. Based on the result of this investigation, we provide you with advice as to what potential risks you run with the use and registration of your trademark idea. We recommend that an investigation is carried out at an early stage of the development of a brand.

If you start using your trademark without research and have to stop this use because a third party successfully objects to it under earlier (trademark) rights, the financial consequences can be enormous. With a trademark search you can avoid this risk as much as possible. For more information about the different types of surveys MarKey offers, please contact us via the contact form at the top right of this page.

Trademark Protection


The trademark clearance and registration is only the first step in successfully managing a brand. It is also important that you actively defend your brand.
Trademark monitoring is a service in which we check daily for you whether new trademarks, which are filed for registration, are identical or similar to your trademark. Brand watch can be set up by country, by region or worldwide. If a possibly conflicting trademark is found, we will inform you about this including an initial indication of the possible actions to be taken.

Why is brand monitoring important:

  • Without trademark monitoring, you will not know whether conflicting trademarks are being applied for for registration.

  • Trademark monitoring allows you to be aware of possibly conflicting trademarks at an early stage which allows you to take timely action against these applications, for example by filing an opposition. This is an administrative and relatively inexpensive objection procedure that can be started at the trademark office and must be submitted before a certain period of time. If you do not make use of this option, you can only act against the registration of an infringing trademark at a later stage by initiating costly legal proceedings.

  • It is important that you actively defend your trademark by taking action against infringing trademarks. If you do not do this, you run the risk that your trademark rights will be diluted and that you will eventually no longer be able to claim exclusive rights to your trademark.

Trademark Infringement, Now What?


Based on your trademark registration, you can take action against third parties who infringe your brands by using a name that is identical or similar to your trademark. On the other hand, you can also be held liable for trademark infringement when you use a trademark that is similar to the trademark of another.

If you discover an infringement of your trademark rights or if you are accused of trademark infringement, it is important to take action as soon as possible and to engage a trademark and design attorney such as MarKey. Fortunately, many disputes can be resolved outside the courtroom, but sometimes going to court is unavoidable.

MarKey advises you on the best route to resolving the conflict in the short term.

Are your trademark rights being infringed? Or are you being accused of infringement? Contact us to discuss the next steps.

Designs
